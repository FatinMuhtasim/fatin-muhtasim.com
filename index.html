My Research

Determination of Optimal Charging Stations for Last Mile Drone Delivery in Windy Condition Using K-Means Clustering Algorithm


Accepted for presentation at 2023 IEEE International Conference on Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management (IEEM2023)


Drone delivery system, a disruptive technology, offers unprecedented ways to ramp up the current delivery efficiency and competitiveness. Specifically, drone delivery system is capable of achieving low delivery times in physically congested metropolitan areas, while reducing the urban pollution associated with delivery service and also improving cost effectiveness. Different research works and implementations of drone delivery system have presented insights into how drone delivery logistics and networks in urban environments will evolve. Among the various factors that affect the drone delivery system, this paper addresses the vital effect of varying wind speeds and establishes a preferable methodology on figuring out an optimized solution for the placement and frequency of charging stations minimizing the overall cost.

Revolutionizing Industry: Unveiling Engineered Bamboo Mass Production for Sustainable Economic Growth in Bangladesh


Under review at The 14th International Conference on Mechanical Engineering (ICME2023)


Bamboo has gained prominence in the research community as a sustainable material due to its eco-friendly nature, affordability, and rapid growth. Over time, researchers have aimed to enhance bamboo's suitability for construction, potentially replacing conventional materials like timber and steel. The solution comes through engineered bamboo, a processed form that addresses the challenges of raw bamboo. This innovative approach has sparked interest globally, leading to large-scale manufacturing in various countries. The South Asia is the main exporter of engineered bamboo, where countries like Bangladesh is far behind. Bangladesh, blessed with a tropical climate, holds immense potential to harness the economic and sustainability benefits of mass-producing engineered bamboo. This research studies the feasibility of producing engineered bamboo in large scale in place of timber. A comprehensive cost-benefit analysis unveils a promising prospect: through this initiative, Bangladesh could contribute a substantial 51.5 million USD annually to its economy, with potential for growth up to 71 million USD through full production optimization. Moreover, this industrialization initiative could markedly reduce 14.8% of total carbon footprint of Bangladesh.

IoT based Smart Jar using NodeMCU ESP8266 and Ultrasonic Sensor


The IoT-based Smart Jar has an ultrasonic sensor (HC05) that enables the detection of content levels inside the jar. To ascertain the degree of fill and the available space, ultrasonic waves are transmitted and reflected. The NodeMCU ESP8266 senses a change in content and then sends the information to a web server. Remote access to this data allows for supply tracking and restocking planning from any place.
